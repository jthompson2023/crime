Russia, sometime in the 1860s. Swirls of liberation, burgeoning science, progressivism, egoism, and nihilism, clamor for attention beneath the towering might of the Russian military elite.<sup>1</sup> St. Petersburg is the captial and microcosm of Russia's contradictions—"the most abstract and premeditated city in the whole world." A towering captial built on a fetid, flooding, alternately sweltering and freezing swamp, because Peter the Great decided Russia needed a port city. Poverty and riches. Order and chaos.
